Does your team see AI as a Tool or a Toy?

System-level knowledge depends on tool-level knowledge

Normally the adoption of tools and techniques takes care of itself, teams organically develop skills and acquire new ones as people join.

That’s a status quo that’s held true for so long we’ve forgotten how to adapt to a faster pace of change. Imagine how hard it would be to create new systems and processes if your team didn’t understand similar tools?

  • Could you have a data strategy if Excel skills weren’t common knowledge?
  • Would app development be possible at a company where only 1 in 10 owned a mobile phone?
  • Remember the challenge of maintaining brand consistency before Canva and Figma?

‘Wait and see’ won’t be a viable AI strategy

We take tool knowledge for granted but when the pace of change is this extreme the adoption of AI skills will be uneven if left to the usual Youtube ‘One weird trick…’ videos and vendor ‘Lunch & Learns’ pace of change.

ChatGPT launched Nov 2022 and could summarise text, today it can complete PhD level research paper tasks and perform tasks as varied as extracting CV details to writing meeting action items. Not since mobile phones or potentially even the internet itself have we seen a technology mature as quickly as AI is currently.

No signs of AI slowing down

AI isn't following the normal S-curve

Normally in tech we see a burst of progress followed by a plateau, a pause that gives us all time to adopt to the new normal.

Researchers looking at AI aren’t seeing any sign of that plateau coming in 2025 or beyond. To quote the AI research firm Epoch AI:

“…To put this 4x annual growth in AI training compute into perspective, it outpaces even some of the fastest technological expansions in recent history.

It surpasses the peak growth rates of:

  • Solar energy capacity installation 1.5x/year
  • Mobile phone adoption 2x/year
  • Human genome sequencing 3.3x/year
